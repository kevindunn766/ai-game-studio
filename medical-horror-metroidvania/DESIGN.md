# Medical Horror Metroidvania — Design Brief (Draft v0.15)

> **Status:** Planning — story and world structure in progress; boss/NPC roster proposed for author review.  
> **Do not implement** until this brief is expanded and approved.

---

## Elevator Pitch

A story-rich medical horror metroidvania set in ancient-styled ruins. The player explores interconnected Greek, Roman, Gothic, and broader ancient environments, gaining powers and meeting enigmatic characters while uncovering the conflict between two foundational figures of medicine.

---

## Genre & Structure

- **Genre:** Medical horror metroidvania
- **Structure:** Interconnected maze of ruins; ability-gated exploration; NPCs, enemies, and bosses advance the narrative and guide progression
- **Map:** ~**100–150 rooms**; each **bone = one room**; full exploration → **zoom out** reveals a **human figure**
- **Limbs** = **architecture types** (symbolic, not literal)
- **Body systems** = **biomes**; **one boss per body system**
- **Hub:** **Village of the Healers** (thoracic center)
- **Nothing is literal — everything is symbolic**

## Story (Recorded — Incomplete)

### Protagonist

- **Female emergency room nurse**
- On her day off when the hiking sequence begins

### Central Conflict

The narrative orbits the **great rift** between:

- **Hippocrates** — the Beneficent
- **Herophilus** — the Great Dissector

### Narrative Shape

- Story-rich; plot and character are first-class
- NPCs, enemies, and bosses lead the main character through the ruin maze
- Enigmatic characters punctuate the journey
- Powers are acquired over the course of exploration and story beats
- Encounters explore the **human condition, morality, ethics, medical history, and mental health**
- **Some encounters have multiple outcomes** that influence future story events

### Opening — Prologue (Recorded)

#### Scene 1: The Empty Room

1. The nurse receives a **call bell from an empty room**.
2. She enters and finds **no patient**.
3. The monitor is beeping; a **heartbeat** is visible on the screen — but the room is empty.
4. She approaches the bed and sees an **impression of a body** on the sheets.
5. She reaches out and is **sucked through a portal**.

#### Scene 2: First Boss — The Great Malaise

- Upon entry to the alternate reality, she must fight the first boss: **the Great Malaise**.

#### Scene 3: False Awakening

- After defeating the Great Malaise, she **awakens as if from a bad dream** in her own bed.
- She rolls over and looks out the window — **everything seems fine**.

#### Scene 4: The Hike

1. The **next morning**, she packs a bag.
2. It is her **day off from work**; she goes on a **hike**.
3. She hikes deep into **forest-covered mountains**.
4. A **storm arrives** faster than she realizes; rain comes within minutes.
5. She runs without knowing which direction she is going.
6. She takes shelter under a **rock overhang** until the rain stops.
7. She realizes she is in a part of the woods she has **never seen** — she is **completely lost**.

#### Scene 5: The Monument — Game Proper Begins

1. While wandering, she pushes through **dense foliage** into a **glade**.
2. A **strange monument** stands in the glade.
3. She **touches the monument**.
4. The **trees morph around her**; she is pulled back into the **alternate reality**.
5. **The game begins here** — this is the true starting point of the metroidvania.

#### Scene 6: The Woods — Wolves, Woodmen, and the Medicine Man

1. The main character wanders through the **woods**.
2. She is **attacked by wolves and woodmen**.
3. In the distance: **overgrown white Greek ruins**.
4. She comes upon a **stranger in the woods** — a **nomad shaman**, a **medicine man**.
5. The medicine man describes an **ancient battle between two great rival medicine men**:
   - One who is **good** — who taught **no harm**
   - The other who **took things apart just to understand how they work**
6. The description is **cryptic**.
7. **Mid-sentence**, the medicine man **rises into the air** and is **torn into many pieces**.
8. His final words float into the air: **"Find the healers"**.

#### Scene 7: The Sword, the Cabin, and the Birth

1. The main character is left to **wander through the woods**.
2. She continues to **fight wolves**.
3. She discovers her **first weapon: a simple sword**.
4. She happens upon the **woodsmen's cabin**, enters, and learns **the true story of Little Red Riding Hood**.
5. She is told to **hunt down the wolf** and **save Little Red Riding Hood**.
6. A short distance away she finds a **pregnant woman** who says she has been **trying to give birth for days**.
7. The woman **wears a wolf skin**.
8. The player must **operate on her to save the baby**.
9. She is sent on a mission into a **cave**, where she finds a **piece of black obsidion**.
10. She **shapes the obsidion into a scalpel** to help the mother give birth.
11. **Player choice — incision direction:**
    - Cut **side to side**, or
    - Cut **top to bottom**
    - One choice **saves the mother**; the other **allows the mother to pass**; **both save the child**.
12. She **brings the baby back to the cabin**.
13. The **woodsman gives her directions to the healer's village**.

#### Scene 8: Outskirts of the Valley

1. Prior to entering the **Village of the Healers**, the main character **wanders the outskirts** for several rooms.
2. She **fights a boss** on the approach (see **Outskirts Boss** in proposed roster).

#### Scene 9: Village of the Healers — Hub

1. The main character enters the **Village of the Healers** — a **ruin**, once glorious, now **shattered by the epic battle** between the two healers.
2. This is the **hub of the map**; from here she experiences the rest of the skeleton-shaped world.
3. She discovers that **most doors are locked** — they will **present themselves** as she explores the map and gains **various items and abilities**.
4. She enters the **anatomical theater** at the **center of town** and **fights a boss** (see **Anatomical Theater Boss** in proposed roster).

## World & Setting

### Framing Reality (Recorded)

- Modern **emergency room**
- **Forest-covered mountains** — hiking trail, storm, rock overhang, unfamiliar woods, glade with monument

### Alternate Reality — Primary Play Space

- **Environment:** Greek / Roman / Gothic / ancient-styled ruins
- **Feel:** Monumental, decayed, medically horrific
- **Layout:** Maze-like interconnected spaces (metroidvania map structure)
- **Entry points (recorded):** ER portal (prologue); monument in the glade (true game start)
- **Early spaces (recorded):** Woods — wolves and woodmen; **overgrown white Greek ruins** visible in the distance
- **Woodsmen's cabin** — true story of Little Red Riding Hood; quest to hunt the wolf; return point for the baby
- **Cave** — source of **black obsidion** for shaping into a scalpel
- **Village of the Healers** (Valley of the Healers) — hub ruin; outskirts approached first; directions given by the woodsman after the birth
- **Anatomical theater** — center of the village; boss encounter
- **Locked doors** throughout the village — open via items/abilities found on the skeleton map

### World Map Structure (Recorded)

| Layer | Meaning | Scale |
|-------|---------|-------|
| **Bone** | One **room** | ~100–150 total |
| **Limb / region** | One **architecture type** | 8 regions (see below) |
| **Body system** | One **biome** + **one boss** | 11 systems (see below) |
| **Full map** | **Human figure** revealed on zoom-out after complete exploration | Macro silhouette |

- **Symbolic rule:** Anatomy is metaphor. A rib room is not a rib — it is a chamber that *feels* like breath, cage, grief, or protection. Architecture and biomes express meaning, not clinical accuracy.
- **Village of the Healers** = hub at the **thoracic center** (sternum / heart vault / forum crossroads).
- **Outskirts rooms** precede hub entry (forest approach — pre-skeleton framing).
- **Zoom-out moment:** After the player has explored the entire map, the camera **pulls back** and the connected rooms resolve into the **shape of a human figure** — the map was a body all along.

### Limb Regions → Architecture (Recorded + Proposed)

Each limb region dictates **building vocabulary** (materials, arches, columns, light). Regions overlap biomes; biomes tint atmosphere within any architecture.

| Region | Bones (rooms) | Architecture | Symbolic register |
|--------|---------------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Head** | Skull, face, mandible (~18 rooms) | **Egyptian** — pylons, judgment halls, mortuary geometry, hieroglyph scars on marble | Identity, memory, witness, fear |
| **Spine** | Cervical → coccyx (~22 rooms) | **Roman** — viaduct spine, aqueduct vertebrae, milestone arches, road-inn alcoves | Continuity, nerve paths, burden, signal |
| **Torso / Hub** | Sternum, ribs, clavicles, scapulae (~24 rooms) | **Greco-Roman** — forum, **anatomical theater**, bath frigidarium, broken odeon | Care, public health, debate, the healer war |
| **Left Arm** | Humerus, radius, ulna, hand (~16 rooms) | **Greek Ionic** — open stoa, oracle colonnades, votive terraces | Reach, oath, intervention |
| **Right Arm** | Humerus, radius, ulna, hand (~16 rooms) | **Roman Imperial** — triumphal arch, catacomb stacks, legionary vaults | Force, procedure, conquest of disease |
| **Pelvis** | Ilium, ischium, pubis, sacrum (~12 rooms) | **Greco-Roman crypt** — birth vaults, cisterns, threshold moats | Origin, consent, inheritance |
| **Left Leg** | Femur, patella, tibia, fibula, foot (~14 rooms) | **Gothic ascent** — nave climbing upward, rose-window fractures, candle drafts | Aspiration, pain climb, hope |
| **Right Leg** | Femur, patella, tibia, fibula, foot (~14 rooms) | **Gothic descent** — undercroft, ossuary aisles, sinking foundations | Grief, descent, reckoning |

**Room budget:** ~136 named bone-rooms (target within 100–150 range; exact bone list can compress or split pairs for pacing).

### Progression & Gating (Proposed)

1. **Forest + outskirts** → Outskirts Boss → **Village hub** (ruined, most doors locked).
2. **Anatomical Theater** boss → reveals map as skeleton; **zoom-out teaser** (partial silhouette).
3. **Body-system bosses** each grant a **Key Virtue** (symbolic item) opening door clusters tied to that system's ethics.
4. **Bone rooms** hold NPCs, folklore encounters, tools, and **choice scars** (persistent world state).
5. **Full exploration** → **final zoom-out** → human figure complete → endgame faction path (Beneficent / Dissector / synthesis).

---

### Symbolic Design Pillars (Recorded)

1. **Architecture = psyche region** — Egyptian head is not Egypt; it is *judgment and memory*.
2. **Biome = moral pressure** — each body system asks a different ethical question.
3. **Blood on marble** persists across all biomes — white/cream stone, red accent as conscience stain.
4. **Folklore is diagnostic** — myths appear as symptoms, not set dressing.
5. **The map is the patient** — zoom-out reveal: the explored world was always a person.

## Encounter Design Principles (Proposed)

1. **Every room is a bone; every bone is symbolic** — rib arches suggest breath and cage, not a anatomy lesson.
2. **Limbs speak in architecture; systems speak in biome** — Gothic leg is descent, not "femur physics."
3. **One boss per body system** — the boss is the biome's ethical thesis; bone rooms carry the argument's verses.
4. **Medical horror over gore for gore's sake** — fear comes from choice, history, and powerlessness.
5. **Branching outcomes** change: locked doors, NPC survival, faction standing, ending path — not just loot.
6. **Zoom-out payoff** — the human figure reveal reframes every prior room as part of one suffering body.
7. **The nurse's ER skills matter** — triage, consent, incision, and bearing witness recur as mechanics and story verbs.

### 01 — Integumentary (Skin) ✓ Draft complete

**Symbolic thesis:** *What covers you is what exposes you.* Skin is the boundary between self and world — shame, dignity, diagnosis written on the surface, the chart that becomes the patient.

**Biome mood:** Parchment halls, flayed banners hung like tapestries, **red ink on cream marble**, drying racks, lint and ash. Air smells of lanolin and iron. Light is flat and clinical — no hiding in shadow; only hiding in plain sight.

**Architecture:** **Egyptian head** (primary) + outer **Greek Ionic** arm galleries (surface / reach). Not literal dermatology — rooms feel like judgment halls where the **face is evidence**.

**Map presence:** 12 bone-rooms · Region: **Head** + **clavicle approaches** · Unlocks from hub after **Anatomical Theater** (door: *The Skin Ledger* — locked until theater boss resolved).

**Hub connection:** Clavicle L `"Span of Vow"` ↔ Sternum `"Forum of Ash"` (hub). Return path opens after boss.
